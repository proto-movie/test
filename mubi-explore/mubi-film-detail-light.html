Wong Kar-wai's luminous meditation on longing and restraint is one of cinema's most sensually precise works. Two neighbours suspect their spouses are having an affair, and find themselves drawn to one another in the charged silence between what is felt and what is said. Every glance and gesture accumulates into something almost unbearable in its beauty.
