About this Course

In this deeply personal masterclass, acclaimed director Wong Kar-wai opens the doors to his singular creative world—a place where time is elastic, emotion is texture, and the camera is an instrument of longing. Drawing from thirty years of making some of cinema's most revered films, he reveals the intuitive, often unexpected processes behind his art.

Over eight episodes, Wong explores the poetic logic of non-linear storytelling, his philosophy of shooting without a script, and how he works with actors to discover truth in the space between words. He shares the obsessive attention to light, colour, and music that defines his visual language—and how patience and uncertainty are not obstacles, but the very source of his films' power.

What You'll Learn
✓ Build a distinctive visual language through colour and light
✓ Work intuitively without a fixed screenplay
✓ Use time and non-linearity as emotional tools
✓ Direct actors through trust and improvisation
✓ Integrate music and sound as narrative layers
✓ Develop a deeply personal cinematic voice

Your Instructor
Wong Kar-wai
One of cinema's most distinctive and poetic voices, Wong Kar-wai has spent four decades redefining what films can feel like. His works — including In the Mood for Love, Chungking Express, and 2046 — are celebrated for their immersive atmospheres, fragmented time, and aching emotional precision. A multiple Cannes laureate, Wong's films are held in the permanent collection of major world cinematheques. In this Academy original, he speaks frankly about the intuition, obsession, and philosophy behind his method.
