How this was extracted: All free-text cancellation responses were clustered into UX sub-themes. Counts shown against sub-themes are the full cluster sizes — not every response in the cluster is exclusively about browse/genre, but the theme is the dominant signal.

Platform data caveat: The cancellation survey has no structured platform field — respondents complete it on the web cancel flow regardless of which device they normally use. Platform tags on quotes below are based on explicit mentions in the response text. Where no platform is named, "Not specified" is shown; those complaints could apply to any surface.

What the survey tells us — and doesn't — about which platforms these complaints hit hardest
The survey doesn't ask "which device were you using?" so we can't produce a clean per-platform complaint count. The table below combines: (a) explicit platform mentions in quote text, (b) known product gaps, and (c) the separate TV & device support cluster (603 complaints) which is the closest proxy for TV-specific browse pain.
🌐
Web
The "browse section" complaint explicitly references web. Web has a Browse page but no Genre / Country / Decade filter rails. Most likely the surface where the misleading catalogue (ghost titles) is most visible, since web shows the broadest catalogue view.
📱
iOS & Android
The "home screen… scrolling infinitely" quote most naturally describes the mobile app home screen. Mobile likely carries the largest share of browse complaints by volume — it's the highest-usage surface — but genre filters are similarly absent on both platforms.
📺
TV apps (Roku, Firestick, Samsung/LG)
TV apps typically offer the most limited browse UI — often just a grid with no filter entry point at all. The separate TV & device support cluster has 603 complaints. TV users who can't find content by genre have the fewest alternatives (no address bar, no easy search) and are most likely to just give up and cancel.
🎮
PS5 / Xbox
No browse/genre complaints explicitly cite console apps in this extract. Console browse UX is broadly similar to TV apps — limited filtering. Likely affected but not called out by name in these responses.

Trust issue
The browse surface includes titles that are not available in the user's region or have already left the catalogue. This is a trust problem — a user who finds "ghost" titles in browse becomes distrustful of the whole catalogue representation.

Design recommendations
Extracted from the full survey analysis — specific to browse and genre discovery
REC 01 — Highest impact
Redesign search and browse with filter rails: Genre, Decade, Country, Director, Language. Surface these as first-class navigation — not buried in an advanced search modal.
REC 02 — Post-signup flow
After a user finishes their signup film, surface a curated "Because you loved X…" genre path within 60 seconds of the credits. The horror/body-horror user who cancelled had no entry point into more like The Substance.
REC 03 — Home screen
Replace the undifferentiated infinite scroll with genre rails (Horror, Documentary, World Cinema, etc.) so users land with orientation rather than a blank scroll prompt.
REC 04 — Catalogue honesty
Filter browse results to only show titles available in the user's region and subscription tier. "Ghost" titles in browse erode trust faster than a smaller-looking catalogue would.
REC 05 — Onboarding
Add a brief taste survey at onboarding (3–5 taps: genre preferences, language, mood) so the homepage rails are personalised from day one rather than generic.
REC 06 — Search empty states
When a search returns no results, propose genre or country browse paths rather than a dead end — "No results for 'Italian films' — try browsing by Country → Italy."
